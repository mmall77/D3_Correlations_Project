Correlations Between Income, Age, Access to Healthcare and Other Factors

Negative Correlations

  • Income vs lack of healthcare
  • The higher the income the lower incidence of smoking
  • The higher the income the lower the incidence of obesity

Positive Correlations

  • Percent of poverty vs. lack of healthcare
  • Poverty vs. both obesity and smoking
  • Age vs. both smoking and obesity

The smoking habit is preponderant in the southern US. Obesity also flourishes in the South . Not surprisingly, the northeastern states in general have the highest income per capita and less obesity and smoking. There is a correlation between median incomes per capita and level of health coverage. MA seems to have one of the highest per capita median incomes with the lowest incidence of people without healthcare. MS has the lowest per capita income of all the states and a high incidence of people without healthcare. TX has a middling income per capita but the highest incidence of non health coverage. The positive relation between poverty and healthcare is the most striking. All the southern states are well represented with high levels of poverty and lack of healthcare.
